I am a PhD student in the School of Mathematical Sciences at Peking University, supervised by Zhifei Zhang and Shengquan Xiang. My current research focuses on the ergodicity and large deviation principle of randomly forced dispersive PDEs.

Topics of Interest
======
**Infinite-Dimensional Random Dynamical Systems**  
Ergodic theory is concerned with the uniqueness of the invariant measure. Heuristically, the mechanism for unique ergodicity is the balance between randomness (spreading out trajectories) and dissipation (concentrating to the origin). New concepts surrounding 'asymptotic compactness' are introduced to handle non-compact state spaces.

**Hyperbolic and Dispersive PDEs**  
To investigate dispersive equations, such as Schrödinger and KdV, we need to combine a number of techniques from harmonic analysis, dynamical systems, control theory, microlocal analysis. In particular, we highlight the role of 'nonlinear smoothing' to overcome the lack of smoothing effect.
